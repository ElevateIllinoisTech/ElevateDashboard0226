Executive Summary

This proposal outlines the pilot curriculum for the Elevate Transfer Student Cohort. The transfer student population at Illinois Tech is distinctive: approximately 200 incoming students per cycle drawn primarily from City Colleges of Chicago, College of DuPage, Moraine Valley Community College, Triton College, and other feeder institutions.

As confirmed in the Transfer Student Engagement Planning meeting, 99% of Year 2 Elevate students are commuters, and transfer students have repeatedly expressed interest in participating in Elevate but have been told the initiative is not yet set up for them. This proposal addresses that gap by creating a self-directed yet coach-supported pathway that recognizes prior experience, accelerates professional development, and integrates transfer students into the Elevate ecosystem.

The structure includes 6 core required activities drawn from the Year 1-2 curriculum (adapted for transfer context), 20 optional transfer-specific activities, and 8 transfer-specific badges mapped to the existing NACE/AAC&U/Kolb/WEF framework alignment.

Design Principles

Recognition of Prior Experience

Transfer students arrive with existing skills, work history, and academic credit. The pathway avoids redundant foundational activities and instead builds on what they bring.

Self-Directed with Coach Support

Per BJ's direction, Spring 2026 is light-touch and self-managed. Fall 2026 full launch includes T&C, Success Coach assignments, and cohort management.

Commuter-Friendly

With 99% of Year 2 students being commuters, all activities must have virtual, asynchronous, or flexible scheduling options.

Accelerated Timeline

Transfer students typically have 2-3 years at Illinois Tech. The pathway compresses the 4-year Elevate experience into an accelerated track.

Strategic Alignment

Gates Foundation Interest: The transfer pathway directly addresses all three Gates Foundation feedback areas — credentialing experiences, integrating academics, and translating prior career learning into credits/badges.

Retention Impact: Transfer student retention is a known institutional challenge. Structured support through Elevate provides the engagement infrastructure that 99% commuter Year 2 students currently lack.

Revenue & Enrollment: Elevate serves as a yield tool for transfer admissions, differentiating Illinois Tech from competitors in the transfer market.

Feeder Pipeline: Strengthens City Colleges, College of DuPage, Moraine Valley, and Triton partnerships. Aligns with Runway 606 cybersecurity/ITM pipeline.

Scalability: This pilot provides the template for eventual graduate student cohort (MBA pilot via Stuart School).

Action Items from Strategy Meeting

Coach Follow-Up Protocol (New — Per BJ Directive)

Every co-curricular plan meeting must be followed by a templated follow-up email within 24 hours. This email should include:

1. Thank-you for meeting and completing the co-curricular plan.
2. Attached copy of the student's co-curricular plan.
3. Clear, concrete next steps specific to their program year (e.g., "Your Year 1 requirements are: Resume Development, Find Your Flock, Experience Completion, Mentorship, and Chicago Adventures").
4. Two optional activities recommended based on their interests and goals.
5. Link to the Elevate portal and relevant 312 checklist items.

Rationale: The co-curricular plan conversation is the entry point to sustained engagement. Students who don't receive clear next steps after this meeting are unlikely to complete additional requirements. This follow-up ensures a baseline service level for all first-year and second-year students.

Elevate Sprint Week (Proposed — Late Spring 2026)

A dedicated 7-10 day sprint where students can check off multiple Elevate requirements in a supported, event-based format. Inspired by the campus tradition of providing meals during exam weeks to drive student engagement. Components include:

Drop-in resume review sessions, LinkedIn/Handshake profile optimization stations, headshot opportunities, mentorship matching, experience reflection support, and badge completion check-ins.

BJ's guidance: Keep initial effort moderate to pilot the concept and gauge student turnout before scaling. Avoid over-investing resources if late-May timing limits participation.

Key Insights & Recommendations

1. Strong Initial Engagement, Low Repeat

Over 91% of check-ins are first-time meetings. Implement structured follow-up cadences with templated emails (mid-semester and end-of-semester touchpoints). Make coach follow-up emails compulsory after every co-curricular plan meeting.

2. Year 2 Engagement Gap

Year 2 engagement (18.3%) trails Year 1 (26.4%) by 8 percentage points. Target sophomore cohort with integration into academic advising and specialized outreach campaigns.

3. Coach Capacity Imbalance

Caseloads range from 1 to 21 students per coach. Rebalancing assignments or implementing a tiered coaching model could improve consistency and scalability.

4. Experience Gap: Job Shadowing Most Desired

56% of students want job shadowing, followed by workshops (50%), volunteering (39%), internships (32%), and research (29%). Create low-barrier structured exposure opportunities before internship commitment.

5. Seasonal Engagement Pattern

October-November represents 78% of Year 1 submissions. Plan a spring semester engagement push with the proposed Elevate Sprint Week and targeted incentives.

6. Engineering Dominance with Niche Interests

Engineering majors comprise 62% of check-ins. Notable niche interests include F1/motorsports (3), game design (4), neuroscience/bioinformatics (2), urban planning (1), and defense engineering (1).

Program Commitment: By signing, students agree to participate in the Elevate initiative administered by Career Services & Experiential Learning at Illinois Institute of Technology.
